# How Google AI Overviews Decide Which Sources to Cite (and How to Be One)

**Category:** AI Search | **Read time:** 8 min | **Published:** 2026-05-06

---

If you've searched Google recently, you've seen them: those blue-shaded answer boxes that appear above all organic results, synthesising information from multiple sources into a single paragraph or list. Google calls them AI Overviews, and they now appear for hundreds of millions of searches daily. For website owners, AI Overviews represent both a threat and an opportunity. A threat because they can reduce clicks to sites that aren't cited. An opportunity because being cited as a source drives authoritative visibility that organic rankings alone can't match.

The question every site owner should be asking in 2026 is not "how do I rank?" but "how does Google decide which sites to cite in AI Overviews?" This article breaks down what we know — and what you can do about it on your Joomla site starting today.

## What Google AI Overviews Actually Are

AI Overviews (formerly Search Generative Experience or SGE) are generated by Google's Gemini model, which is trained on a vast corpus of web content and integrated directly with Google's real-time index. Unlike a chatbot, AI Overviews don't just retrieve a single source — they synthesise information across multiple pages and then surface links to their sources.

The sources that appear in AI Overviews are not the same as the top organic results. Google's model evaluates content for:

- **Factual accuracy** — is the claim verifiable and consistent with other trusted sources?
- **Depth and specificity** — does the page go beyond surface-level explanation?
- **Structured data** — does the page help Google understand its content unambiguously?
- **E-E-A-T signals** — is there evidence of experience, expertise, authority, and trustworthiness?
- **Freshness** — is the content up to date?

A page can rank on page 2 of organic results and still be cited in an AI Overview. Conversely, a page ranking #1 organically may never appear in AI Overviews if it lacks the depth and structure the model needs.

## The Signals That Put You in AI Overviews

### 1. Structured Data (Schema.org)

This is the most actionable signal for most site owners. Google's AI system uses structured data to understand what a page is about without having to infer it from text alone. Pages with complete, correct Schema.org markup are more likely to be cited because the model can confidently attribute specific facts to them.

Key schema types that improve your chances:

- **Article / BlogPosting** — with `author`, `datePublished`, `dateModified`, `headline`
- **FAQPage** — FAQ schema can pull individual Q&A pairs directly into AI Overviews
- **HowTo** — step-by-step content with numbered steps in schema format
- **LocalBusiness** — for location-based queries
- **Product** — with `offers`, `aggregateRating`

If your pages have no schema at all, you're invisible to the structured layer that AI Overviews rely on.

### 2. Direct Answer Format

AI Overviews favour content that clearly states its answer near the top of the page. If someone searches "how long does it take to get a Schengen visa?", a page that opens with "The processing time for a Schengen visa is typically 15 calendar days" is much more likely to be cited than a page that buries the answer after three paragraphs of context.

**Practical implication:** Lead with your answer. Put the key fact or conclusion first, then provide supporting detail.

### 3. Authoritativeness and E-E-A-T

Google's guidelines for quality evaluation — E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) — directly influence which sources the AI model trusts. Pages with named authors who have documented credentials, citations from other respected sources, and a clear "about" structure are rated more trustworthy.

For Joomla site owners this means:
- Add author bio pages and link articles to their authors using `Article` schema
- Include a clear "About" page with your organisation's background
- Link to primary sources within your content
- Mark up your organisation with `Organization` schema including your official name, logo, and contact details

### 4. Content Freshness

AI Overviews consistently favour recent content for time-sensitive queries. A 2023 article about AI search trends is less likely to be cited for a 2026 query than a 2026 article covering the same topic. Keep your key pages updated with a `dateModified` property in your Article schema so Google knows when you last reviewed the content.

### 5. Page Quality Signals

Pages that load slowly, have excessive ads, or display interstitial popups are deprioritised. Core Web Vitals — Largest Contentful Paint, Cumulative Layout Shift, and Interaction to Next Paint — feed directly into Google's quality assessment. A fast, clean page is a prerequisite, not a nice-to-have.

## What Makes Google Skip a Source

Understanding exclusions is as valuable as understanding inclusions:

- **`noindex` pages** — never crawled or cited, even with excellent content. Check that staging pages aren't accidentally public.
- **Blocked crawlers** — if `robots.txt` blocks Googlebot or `Google-Extended`, those pages are excluded. Blocking `Google-Extended` is a legitimate choice, but it reduces AI Overview eligibility.
- **Thin content** — pages under ~300 words are rarely cited. Substantive, specific pages are preferred.
- **Duplicate content** — if Google flags your page as a near-duplicate, the other version is cited instead.
- **Broken structured data** — schema with critical errors (wrong type, invalid JSON) can send contradictory signals and reduce trust.

## A Practical Action Plan for Joomla Site Owners

**1. Audit your schema coverage.** Use Google's Rich Results Test or Search Console to find which pages have schema and which don't. Prioritise your homepage, service pages, and top articles.

**2. Add Article schema to every blog post.** Include at minimum: `headline`, `author` (with `name`), `datePublished`, `dateModified`, and `publisher`. These let the AI model attribute claims to a named author — a key trust signal.

**3. Add FAQPage schema to FAQ content.** This is one of the most direct routes into AI Overviews — the model can extract individual Q&A pairs and cite them directly in responses.

**4. Lead with direct answers.** Rewrite your key pages so the opening paragraph states the answer clearly. The conclusion or key fact should appear first; supporting detail follows.

**5. Check your crawlability.** Confirm Googlebot isn't blocked in `robots.txt` and key pages don't carry `<meta name="robots" content="noindex">`.

**6. Improve page speed.** Aim for LCP under 2.5 seconds on mobile. Enable Joomla caching, compress images, and use a CDN if traffic warrants it.
